A bit About David Sairai

Who Am I

My name is David Sairai and I am originally from Zimbabwe and been a resident citizen of the city of Cape Town since 2012. I currently work as an IT Support Engineer for a UK-based software company in Stellenbosch and have worked in various IT roles in Johannesburg, Cape Town and Harare my home city.

I had a weird start to my IT career. Started off at a Catholic boarding school called St Ignatius with ZX Spectrum and commodore (thank you, Fr. Wolfe). Then when varsity fell short as I didn tmeet the required grades to do a degree, I moved into the employment field in the fast food area, then moved “up” the ladder to being a cashier at various retail stores.

Meanwhile, I was busy studying in IT part-time at night at a college in town when one day during my lunch break as I was doing my college homework, a gentleman came in at my workplace to buy some electrical cable. He asked me what I was doing, and I replied that I was doing my college homework. We had a brief chat and after seeing my college homework and IT textbooks, he said he has an opening at his company and if I am interested to join his company as a IT Software Assistant. Suffice to say, I resigned from that job the following day to enter the IT world.

I started to my work in Linux and Windows 3.1, then moved i to various SysAdmin roles for various companies, including some public listed companies where I got involved and learnt various software, platforms and applications. I then bounced down under to SA when I got a job in Johannesburg where I travelled all over SA overseeing the rollout of a Microsoft Retail POS system in various retail outlets. IN 2012, I public listed comany headed hunted me on Linkedin and I relocated to Cape Town where i worked for five years befoe moving to an american global IT company for another two years where I learnt a lot about being humble, disciplined and pushing yourself to make your own career. This year I moved to Stellenbosch to a new role where I am currently at.

My Roadmap to Studying

In 2021, I enrolled for the Diploma in Information Technology through the University of South Africa as I wanted to add a formal qualification. Even though I have been certifying myself in various vendor based certifications in the past ten years of my IT career, a formal qualification will enable me to proceed doing a degree in Information Technology hopefully through the same university. God willing, I would want to continue to add the Masters qualification thereafter as this will give me an added advantage to get into managerial roles in the corporate world.
